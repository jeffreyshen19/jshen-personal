Hey, I'm Jeffrey, a student, technologist, and interdisciplinary researcher. My work uses software development, machine learning, data visualization, cartography, and writing to clarify and expose the sociopolitical impacts of technology.

I recently graduated from MIT with a degree in computer science and minor in political science, and am an incoming full-stack web developer at the U.S. Census Bureau. Next fall, I will begin my Masters in Engineering in the MIT Digital Humanities Lab and the Human Dynamics Group in the MIT Media Lab. On campus, I also help run a reading group focused on understanding contemporary social issues, and spend a lot of my time thinking, talking, and (hopefully) writing about politics, history, and urbanism.

Please reach out via email or Twitter!

Research

2020-2022

The Cop In Your Neighbor's Doorbell: Amazon Ring And The Spread of Participatory Mass Surveillance

Used spatial regression modeling and computational grounded theory to study how Amazon's Ring Neighbors platform is used nationally and, as a case study, in Los Angeles. Our paper was accepted for CSCW 2022, with a preprint available. Research was conducted in the MIT Media Lab's Human Dynamics group, with Dan Calacci and Alex (Sandy) Pentland.

Data Visualization

Summer 2021

ShotSpotter in Kansas City, MO

An interactive, data visual article on how ShotSpotter acoustic gunshot detection technology is used to faciliate urban "renewal" and displacement. Work supported by the 2020-2021 Human Rights & Technology Fellowship at the MIT Center for International Studies.

Blog

Amazon Ring's Police Partnership Tracker

Politics

LobbyView

Worked with the MIT Political Science Department, developed interactive data visualizations using D3 and React for LobbyView, a database of Congressional lobbying that aims to facilitate political science research and bring transparency to money in politics.

2018

Is My District Gerrymandered?

Created an interactive website that estimates how gerrymandered a U.S. congressional district is, using geometry and election results. Project won the 2018 Congressional App Challenge in Massachusetts's 6th District.

Exploring the Seats-Votes Curve

Developed an interactive, data-driven explainer of the seats-votes curve, a measure of fairness in redistricting. Built with D3.
